## Why You Need an Administrative Lawyer in Oaxaca

**Administrative Law in Oaxaca** regulates the relationship between citizens and government agencies. When an authority acts illegally or arbitrarily, you have the right to challenge that resolution. However, deadlines are extremely short (often 15 days), procedures are highly technical, and an error can cost you the case. Having an **Administrative Lawyer in Oaxaca de Juárez** with experience before the **Administrative Justice Tribunal (TJAEO)** and Federal District Courts is the difference between losing your business or reclaiming your rights.

At the law firm of **Karina Orocio Cruz**, we defend clients across the State of Oaxaca, including **Salina Cruz**, **Huajuapan**, **Juchitán**, and **Tuxtepec**, against government overreach.

## Our Administrative Law Services in Oaxaca

### Business Closure Lifting (Clausuras)
A sudden closure can destroy months of work. If municipal or state authorities (Gobernación, PROFECO, IMSS, etc.) have closed your business in Oaxaca, we act immediately:
- **Nullity Lawsuit**: Filed before the TJAEO to declare the closure illegal.
- **Amparo Proceedings**: To seek an immediate "Suspension" from a federal judge, allowing you to reopen while the trial continues.
- **Urgent Precautionary Measures**: Protecting your source of income from day one.

### Nullification of Traffic Fines and Sanctions
Traffic fines imposed by municipal or state police often lack legal foundation or follow improper procedures. We challenge:
- Municipal and state traffic fines.
- Sanchions imposed by IMSS or INFONAVIT on employers.
- Sanctions from the STPS (labor audits).
- Arbitrary fines from the SAT or local tax authorities.

### Amparo Lawsuits against Authority Acts
The **Amparo Proceeding** is the most powerful constitutional tool in Mexico to stop illegal government acts. We handle amparos in the Federal District Courts of Oaxaca to:
- Stop illegal demolishments or asset seizures.
- Challenge administrative regulations that affect your business.
- Recover confiscated goods or properties seized without due process.

### Government Contracts & Licitations
We advise companies on competing for public works and service contracts in the State of Oaxaca:
- Drafting successful technical and economic proposals.
- Challenging unfair disqualifications through "Inconformidad" appeals.
- Claiming pending payments and cost adjustments from government agencies.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long do I have to challenge a fine or closure?**
Deadlines are very short: generally **15 business days** for administrative appeals or amparos, and **45 business days** for nullity lawsuits. If you miss these windows, the act becomes final and cannot be challenged.

**Can I reopen my business while the trial lasts?**
Yes. Through a "Suspension" request (Stay of execution), we can often get a judge to allow the business to reopen while the legality of the closure is being decided in court.

**Does the firm handle cases outside of Oaxaca City?**
Yes. We represent clients throughout the State of Oaxaca. We use virtual consultations and digital filing to maintain efficiency regardless of your location.
