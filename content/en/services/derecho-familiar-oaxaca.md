## Our Family Law Services in Oaxaca

### No-Fault Divorce (Express Divorce) in Oaxaca
Since the reform of the Civil Code of Oaxaca, **no-fault divorce** allows for the dissolution of marriage unilaterally, without having to prove "grounds" or the fault of the other spouse. This process is faster and less confrontational when there are agreements regarding children and assets.
- **Unilateral Divorce**: When only one party wants the divorce.
- **Mutual Consent Divorce**: When both parties agree; faster and more affordable.
- **Liquidation of Marital Assets**: Inventary and fair distribution of property acquired during marriage.

### Child Support (Pensión Alimenticia)
**Child support in Oaxaca** is the right of children, spouses, or partners to receive economic resources for basic needs: food, clothing, housing, health, and education. We act firmly to ensure:
- Initial child support lawsuits.
- **Provisional Child Support**: Immediate measures to guarantee payment from the start of the trial.
- Increases or reductions in support based on changing circumstances.
- Forced execution when the debtor fails to pay (seizure of salary/accounts).

### Guarding, Custody, and Visitation
**Custody in Oaxaca** defines who provides the day-to-day care of the minor.
- **Sole Custody**: One parent has guarding; the other has visitation rights.
- **Joint Custody**: Both parents share time with the children, a model increasingly favored by Oaxacan judges when it serves the child's best interest.
We draft comprehensive visitation and custody agreements that prioritize the psychological stability of the minors.

### Recognition of Paternity
If a father does not voluntarily recognize his child, we initiate the **Paternity Recognition Trial** in Oaxaca with DNA testing requests, judicial resolution, and birth certificate updates at the Civil Registry.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long does a divorce take in Oaxaca?**
A mutual consent divorce with an agreement can be resolved in **1 to 3 months**. A unilateral process without prior agreement can take between 6 and 18 months depending on the complexity of assets and custody.

**Does a common-law partner (concubina) have rights in Oaxaca?**
Yes. The Civil Code of Oaxaca recognizes rights for common-law partners who have lived together for at least two years or have children together. This includes rights to child support and potentially a share of common property.

**Can a father request joint custody in Oaxaca?**
Absolutely. Oaxacan judges apply the principle of the **best interest of the child**. If both parents are fit, joint custody is a viable and frequent option in our courts.

**How do I ensure payment if the father refuses to pay child support?**
We can request the seizure of salary directly with the employer, the blocking of bank accounts, or the seizure of property. In extreme cases, repeated non-payment is a criminal offense in Oaxaca.
