## Frequently Asked Questions About Legal Services in Tehuantepec

**What do I do if a family member died without a will and there are properties in Tehuantepec?**
We initiate an intestate probate proceeding. The death certificate, birth certificates of potential heirs, and property documentation must be presented. The judge will appoint an executor and, after the inventory, the assets will be adjudicated to the lawful heirs.

**How do I resolve a boundary dispute with my neighbor?**
We first attempt out-of-court conciliation. If no agreement is reached, we file a boundary demarcation proceeding before the civil court. The court orders a judicial inspection and, based on the expert opinion, sets the definitive boundaries.

**How much does a probate proceeding cost in Tehuantepec?**
Fees depend on the value of the assets. We offer affordable plans and work with cadastral appraisals to minimize costs. The first consultation is free, and we give you an exact quote then.

**Can I regularize a property I bought 15 years ago without title in Tehuantepec?**
Yes. If you have possessed the property peacefully for more than 5 years, you can file for adverse possession (usucapión). The process requires proving possession with receipts, witnesses, or purchase agreements. We guide you through the entire process.

**What is the difference between a will and an intestate succession?**
A will is a voluntary act during life where you designate heirs. Intestate succession occurs when someone dies without a will, and the law determines who inherits. We always recommend making a will to save your family time and money.
