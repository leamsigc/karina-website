## Frequently Asked Questions About Legal Services in Tlaxiaco

**How long must I possess a piece of land to claim adverse possession in Tlaxiaco?**
The law requires 5 years of continuous, peaceful, and public possession for urban properties. You must demonstrate that you have acted as owner: paying property tax, making improvements, or having witnesses. We gather all the evidence for the proceeding.

**What do I do if my birth certificate has my surname spelled wrong?**
We file a rectification request before the Civil Registry judge. Your current certificate, official documents showing the correct name, and two witnesses are required. The administrative process can be resolved in 1 to 2 months.

**How much does a divorce cost in Tlaxiaco?**
A no-fault divorce has affordable costs. It includes filing the petition, serving your spouse, and obtaining the dissolution of marriage judgment. We give you an exact no-obligation quote during the free consultation.

**Can I regularize an ejido property in Tlaxiaco?**
Ejido properties require a special procedure before the Agrarian Unitary Court. If your land is no longer collectively farmed and you possess it individually, we can file for agrarian adverse possession.

**What documents do I need to start a civil registry correction?**
You need the original certificate with the error, your official ID, your children's birth certificates (if applicable), and any document showing the correct information (voter ID, passport, professional license). We guide you through every step.
