::CaseStudyDetailContent
---
challenge: "Several families in the Isthmus of Tehuantepec region had signed long-term (30-year) lease contracts with transnational wind energy companies under highly disadvantageous terms, with royalty payments far below market value and clauses severely limiting the use of their remaining lands."

strategy: "We conducted a thorough legal audit of the original contracts, identifying defects in consent and one-sided clauses. We initiated an out-of-court renegotiation process with the warning of filing lawsuit for contract nullification due to lesion and economic disproportion."

outcome: "We got the transnational company to the negotiation table, resulting in the signing of amendment agreements. The abusive restrictive clauses were eliminated and a 40% increase in economic considerations (royalties) was achieved in favor of the landowners, ensuring fair treatment for the use of their lands."
