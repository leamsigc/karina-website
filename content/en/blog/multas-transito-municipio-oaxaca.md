## Your Rights Against the Traffic Authority

Receiving a traffic ticket in the Municipality of Oaxaca de Juárez can be a frustrating experience. However, it is important for you to know that many of these fines lack the minimum legal requirements to be valid. In this article, we explain how you can defend yourself and what authority errors can save your wallet.

### Most Common Grounds for Annulment

For a fine to be legal, the traffic officer must strictly comply with what is dictated by the **Traffic Regulation for the Municipality of Oaxaca de Juárez**. If the officer commits any of the following errors, the fine can be declared null by a judge:

1.  **Lack of Legal Grounds and Motivation:** It is not enough to say "ran a red light." The officer must cite the exact article, section, and subsection of the regulation and describe in detail the circumstances of **Time, Manner, and Place**. If the ticket is vague, it is illegal.
2.  **Omission of Signatures or Identification:** If the record is not duly signed by the officer or if they did not fully identify themselves at the time of the violation, the act loses validity.
3.  **Illegal Document Retention:** In Oaxaca, officers **cannot retain your license, registration card, or license plates** as a guarantee of payment. This practice is unconstitutional and a direct cause for annulment.

### The Nullity Trial: Your Legal Weapon

Many people choose to pay the fine with a discount to "avoid problems." What few know is that you have the right to file a **Nullity Trial** before the **Administrative Justice Court of the State of Oaxaca (TJAO)**.

*   **The Golden Deadline:** You have only **15 business days** from the time you receive the fine to file your lawsuit. If you let this time pass, the fine becomes final and mandatory.
*   **Can the money be recovered?** Yes. If you decide to pay the fine to recover your vehicle or avoid late fees, you can still challenge it. If you win the trial, the Municipality of Oaxaca de Juárez is obliged to return the money to you.

### Quick Guide: What to Do When Receiving a Fine?

1.  **Do not sign if you do not agree:** You are not obliged to accept guilt at the moment.
2.  **Take evidence:** Photos of the place, signs (or lack thereof), and the patrol/officer number.
3.  **Review the ticket:** Verify that the date and location are correct. Any error in the address can be grounds for annulment.
4.  **Consult an expert:** Administrative law is technical. A specialized lawyer can identify form defects that go unnoticed by the average citizen.

### Conclusion

Fighting a traffic fine is not just a matter of money; it is a matter of legality. Municipal authorities must be the first to respect the regulations they swore to defend.
