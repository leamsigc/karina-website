## Frequently Asked Questions About Legal Services in Pochutla

**What do I do if the municipality closes my business without cause?**
We file an annulment appeal before the Administrative Justice Court. The closure must be based on a valid municipal regulation and properly notified. If due process was not followed, we obtain its revocation and damages compensation.

**How do I regularize a property I bought years ago in Pochutla?**
If you have possessed the property peacefully, continuously, and publicly for more than 5 years, you can request adverse possession (usucapión). We handle the entire process before the civil court until the judgment and deed are obtained in your name.

**How long does a child support case take on the Costa?**
The proceeding can be resolved in 2 to 4 months if the defendant has verifiable income. The judge sets a percentage of the salary, and we can request payroll deduction to ensure timely payment.

**Can I sue a municipal authority for damage to my property?**
Yes. When the municipality performs works or administrative acts that affect your property without proper compensation, we can file a state liability claim before the competent courts.

**What do I need to start a divorce in Pochutla?**
You only need your marriage certificate, official ID, and a declaration of your intent to divorce. No cause is required. No-fault divorce is processed quickly and without confrontation.
